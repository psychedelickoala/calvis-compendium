What is the prime \(k\)-tuple conjecture?

Let \(h\) denote a constant natural number, then let \(K\) be a set of constant natural numbers \(\{0, a_2, a_3, ..., a_h\}\). Let \(n\) be prime generating on K if \(\{n, n+a_2, n+a_3, ..., n+a_h\}\) is a set of primes. Have \(d(N)\) return the density of primes below \(N\), and let \(d_K(N)\) return the density of prime-generating \(n\) below \(N\).

Hardy and Littlewood's prime \(k\)-tuple conjecture states that \[d_K(N)\sim C_K\cdot d(N)^{h}\] where \(C_K\) is given by the convergent product \[\prod_{p'=2}^{\infty} \frac{1-w_K(p)\cdot p^{-1}}{\left(1-p^{-1}\right)^h},\] indexed by prime \(p\) as indicated by the notation \(p'\). The function \(w_K(p)\) gives the number of unique elements in \(\{0, a_2\bmod{p}, a_3\bmod{p}, ..., a_h\bmod{p}\}\).
