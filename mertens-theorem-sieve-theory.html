Mertens' theorem and sieve theory

Mertens' third theorem states that \[\lim_{N\to\infty}\ln{N}\prod_{p'=2}^{N}\left(1-\frac{1}{p}\right)=e^{-\gamma},\] and by the Prime Number Theorem, the density of primes below \(N\), denoted \(d(N)\), has asymptotic behaviour \[d(N)\sim\frac{1}{\ln{N}};\] then by the transitive property, it is known that \[d(N)\sim e^{\gamma}\prod_{p'=2}^{N}\left(1-\frac{1}{p}\right).\]

Consider the natural numbers from \(1\) to \(N\) inclusive. Removing all multiples of each prime \(p\) for which \(2 \le p\le \sqrt{N}\) leaves only primes remaining. Note that \(1\) is considered prime. Let \(R_p(N)\) return the ratio: \[\frac{#n\le N \text{ s.t. } \forall k'\le p, k\not\mid n} {#n\le N \text{ s.t. } \forall k'< p, k\not\mid n}\]
