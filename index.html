Welcome to Calvi's Compendium!

Hello and welcome to my blog! I am a high school student with an interest in mathematics. Here I will primarily discuss elementary number theory, but might branch out into other topics like geometry and logic.

For around a year I have been working on famous open problems such as Riemann's Hypothesis and the Twin Prime Conjecture. Many times I thought I'd solved one of them, only to find relatively obvious errors in my proofs shortly afterward. By now, I have accepted that these problems don't want to be solved using basic sieving methods or high school algebra. Quite inconsiderate of them, really. :(

Harsh realities aside, the most important thing I learned was that there is more to mathematics than solving famous problems; ultimately, mathematical problems are only a starting point for understanding the behaviour of mathematical objects, such as the zeta function or the prime numbers. In my experience, an ambitious focus on problem solving often resulted in my purposefully disregarding the many 'side quests' that presented themselves, in favour of moving along with my proof. Consequently, I deprived myself of what could have been many fascinating pieces of mathematics.

That's what this blog is for: a compendium of side quests. My main goal is to build an understanding of primes, and prime sequences; and also maybe to branch out, and explore other topics. Perhaps I'll stumble across an original idea, or cultivate a unique perspective on some classic concept. Perhaps I'll achieve neither of these things. If nothing else, I should become more fluent in \(\LaTeX\), and hopefully improve my mathematical writing.
