Infinite descent in prime sieves

The following animation was made with the manim library for Python.

For each prime \(p\), let \(p\)-primes be natural numbers \(n\) such that: \[\forall k'\le p, k\not\mid n;\] and let \(p\)-mines be natural \(n\) satisfying: \[\forall k' < p, k\not\mid n \land p\mid n\].

To discuss infinite descent and explain the above animation, I need to denote the highest prime below \(p\) and the lowest prime above \(p\). These can be written as \(\triangleleft p\) and \(p \triangleright \) respectively.
